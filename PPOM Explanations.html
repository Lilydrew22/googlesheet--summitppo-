AetnaAetna only allows ONE fee schedule per address regardless of the TIN/Providers contracted. This means that they do not allow "Split Participation", meaning one provider at the location cannot be direct while a different provider participates under a leasing network. All providers at the location must participate in-network the same way.

Additionally, in order to optimize Aetna from either a direct contract or from one leasing network to a new leasing relationship, this means you must first terminate your contract or current participation completely. Then Aetna reviews the provider files and adds providers under the leasing relationships available. This means you will spend time out of network before Aetna will add an office/provider under the leasing relationship. They will also send letters to their members notifying them of your termination.

If you choose to move from a direct contract to a leasing network, you will not be in-network for all Aetna patients. They only lease their PPO II patients and are not forthcoming about which patient groups fall under this network. 		Notes about IRR/Negotiations

Anthem/Unicare (Plan optimization)Anthem has multiple plans/networks within their company. The 100, 200, & 300 are tier fee schedules which are the various plan levels that you may choose to accept. Some fee tier levels may have the same fees depending on the area.

You must decide if you would like to continue to participate for all Anthem patients. If you want to be in-network for all Anthem patients, then you will remain contracted and paid on their lowest tier fees.

For example, if you only chose to shift your participation to the 300 tier, you will be considered Out Of Network for the 100 and 200 tier plans. If you choose to be with more than 1 plan level, you will always be reimbursed on the lowest plan you are participating with. So, if you are participating with plans 200 and 300 all Anthem plans will be reimbursed on the 200 level fees.

There is only one level of participation for specialists. Specialists will be in-network for all patients (on the Levels 100/200/300) but are paid on the higher tier fees.

Cigna (Direct to Network)Cigna has two plans: Cigna DPPO Advantage and Cigna DPPO. If you are participating with Cigna on their Advantage plan, you are in-network for all patients and are reimbursed the same fees for all patients. While participation in the DPPO network allows an office/provider to see all Cigna patients, Cigna limits the fee (amount Cigna will pay) for a DPPO Advantage member. You may see differences in reimbursements based on the actual plan limits for the Advantage member.

The Cigna DPPO network does fall under the leasing relationships. However, optimization with Cigna can take a very long time, especially after a direct contract termination and they will send letters to their patients advising them of your termination. Additionally, Cigna reviews the leasing network reimbursements and compares it to the office direct contract fees. If the intended network fee schedule is too high, such as a large gap from the original direct fees, or the leasing network fee schedule is unreasonably high, Cigna will block adding the provider under the leasing network. This means that you will remain completely out of network.

If you are looking to move this participation, you will need to call Cigna (Provider Relations: 800-244-6224 (provider, TIN, contracting, dental)) to ask about your participation or send us a redacted EOB so we can review your participation.

Delta (If eligible for Premier Only)Your state's Delta allows you to move to Premier only. As a Delta Premier only provider when you:
1) treat a PREMIER patient, the dentist will receive reimbursement based on the Premier Maximum Plan Allowance (MPA). The patient will be responsible for paying any copayment, deductible or non-covered service. These dentists cannot charge the difference between this fee and their regular office fees. They can only collect the Premier MPA.

2) treat a PPO patient, the dentist will receive reimbursement from Delta Dental based on the PPO MPA. The patient will be responsible for paying the difference of that fee up to the Premier MPA; plus any copayment, deductible or non-covered service. There are exceptions to this - patients who have an “Exclusive” PPO plan which states they have NO benefits unless they are treated by a PPO dentist. These plans are not prominent but they do exist. Some TIAA-CREF employees have this type of plan as well as T-Mobile and others (not an all-inclusive list).

3) treat a PPO+PREMIER patient, the dentist will receive reimbursement based on the Premier MPA. The patient will be responsible for paying any copayment, deductible or non-covered service.
